main courses

  • Grilled Chicken with Steamed Vegetables

    Lean chicken breast grilled with simple spices like pepper and garlic. Served with steamed broccoli, carrots, or green beans. It's high in protein, low in fat, and very filling.

  • Baked Salmon with Quinoa

    Salmon is rich in omega-3 healthy fats. Baking it keeps it moist without adding oil. Paired with quinoa (a protein-rich whole grain), this dish is nutritious and great for heart health.

  • Vegetable Stir-Fry with Brown Rice

    Mixed vegetables such as bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, and snap peas cooked quickly in a small amount of olive oil or soy sauce. Served with brown rice to add fiber. Light, colorful, and packed with vitamins.

desserts

  • Fruit Salad with Honey-Lemon Dressing

    A mix of colorful fruits such as strawberries, kiwi, oranges, apples, and grapes. A small drizzle of honey and lemon adds extra flavor. Naturally sweet, full of vitamins, and very refreshing.

  • Greek Yogurt Parfait

    Layers of Greek yogurt, fresh berries, and a little granola. High in protein and antioxidants. It feels like a treat but stays very healthy.

  • Chia Seed Pudding

    Chia seeds soaked in milk (or almond milk) until they thicken into a pudding-like texture. Sweetened lightly with honey and topped with fruit. High in fiber and great for digestion.
